Executive Summary

This plan outlines the formation of a formal nonprofit foundation to support the Human Flourishing Frameworks (HFF) project. HFF is currently a privacy-first AI chat and media curation system at TRL 4, with components including local AI chat, media libraries, and research frameworks for measuring human flourishing outcomes.

Foundation Type: 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization

Primary Mission: Advance human flourishing through privacy-preserving AI research, open-source development, and evidence-based measurement frameworks that respect individual autonomy while enabling collective progress

Jurisdiction: United States (Ohio, based on operator location)

Mission Statement

To advance human flourishing through privacy-preserving artificial intelligence research, open-source technology development, and evidence-based measurement frameworks that respect individual autonomy while enabling collective progress.

Vision Statement

A world where AI tools enhance human well-being through transparent, local-first, and evidence-based approaches that prioritize privacy, agency, and measurable positive outcomes.

Core Values

  • Privacy-First: Default to local, user-controlled systems
  • Evidence-Based: Claims backed by validation and measurable outcomes
  • Open Source: Public benefit through accessible technology
  • Human Agency: Tools that augment, not replace, human judgment
  • Transparency: Clear boundaries, limitations, and uncertainty communication
  • Inclusivity: Accessible across ability, economic, and geographic boundaries

Intellectual Property Strategy

Open Source Licensing Framework

Core Components:

  • HFF Framework: AGPL-3.0 (strong copyleft for networked software)
  • Lantern OS Components: MIT/Apache-2.0 (permissive for broader adoption)
  • Documentation: CC-BY-SA-4.0 (share-alike for knowledge)
  • Art/Media: CC-BY-4.0 or CC0 (public domain where appropriate)

Dual Licensing Model:

  • Commercial licensing for enterprise features/support

Patent Policy

Legal and Compliance Requirements

Formation Steps

1. Ohio Nonprofit Incorporation:

  • File Articles of Incorporation with Ohio Secretary of State
  • Include required 501(c)(3) language
  • $99 filing fee (Ohio)

2. EIN Acquisition:

  • Obtain Employer Identification Number from IRS
  • Required for bank account, tax filings, hiring

3. 501(c)(3) Application:

  • File IRS Form 1023 (expedited review available)
  • $600 filing fee ($275 for small organizations)
  • 3-12 month typical processing time

4. State Tax Exemption:

  • Ohio sales tax exemption
  • Ohio property tax exemption (if applicable)
  • State charitable solicitation registration

5. Ongoing Compliance:

  • Annual Ohio biennial report ($25)
  • IRS Form 990 annual filing
  • State charitable registration renewals
  • Corporate minutes and board meeting records

Risk Management

Key Risks and Mitigations

1. Funding Risk:

  • Risk: Insufficient fleet/fan support to sustain operations
  • Mitigation: Gradual scaling, community engagement, bootstrap approach

2. Legal Liability:

  • Risk: Software misuse, privacy breaches, IP claims
  • Mitigation: Strong licenses, disclaimers, insurance coverage, legal counsel

3. Governance Challenges:

  • Risk: Board conflicts, founder burnout, mission drift
  • Mitigation: Clear bylaws, term limits, conflict policies, succession planning

4. Technical Debt:

  • Risk: Code quality issues, security vulnerabilities
  • Mitigation: Regular audits, security reviews, contribution guidelines

5. Community Management:

  • Risk: Toxic community, contributor burnout, harassment
  • Mitigation: Code of conduct, moderation policies, inclusive practices

6. Fleet Coordination Risk:

  • Risk: Fleet resource conflicts, revenue disputes
  • Mitigation: Clear governance, transparent sharing, dispute resolution

Insurance Coverage

  • Directors and Officers (D&O) Insurance: Board liability protection
  • General Liability: Physical and operational risks
  • Cyber Liability: Data breaches, privacy incidents
  • Professional Liability: Errors and omissions in services
