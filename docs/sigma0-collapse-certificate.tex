\title{\textbf{$\Sigma_0$ --- The Collapse Certificate}\\[4pt]
\large A computable stability certificate for convergence dynamics, and a
demonstration of why an ungrounded self-improving system collapses}
\author{Lantern OS \\ \texttt{src/cio\_sde/}}
\date{\today}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
We give a computable certificate for collapse in a dissipative nonlinear
system: splitting the local Jacobian's symmetric part into active and
near-null subspaces yields a Lyapunov function $V(x)=\tfrac12\norm{\PM x}^2$
whose decay is guaranteed exactly when the active spectral abscissa is
negative. We pair this with a semantic collapse operator $\Szero$ (projection
onto the null manifold --- the ``42-state'') and an anti-collapse operator
$\Sigma_0^{-1}$ (persistent excitation along null modes), and a critical-slowing
``canary'' early-warning scalar. Running the construction on a real
conversation log, two independent methods --- local Jacobian fitting and a
reservoir reconstruction of the global flow --- converge on the same
\emph{parrot attractor}, an in-data instance of model collapse. We read the
result as a small, honest warning about recursively self-improving systems:
absent external grounding, the dynamics admit only collapse or divergence.
All theorems are verified against rollouts ($20/20$ tests passing); the data
results are explicitly labelled illustrative.
\end{abstract}

\section{The object}
We study a dissipative nonlinear system
\begin{equation}
\dot{x} = f(x,u,\theta), \qquad x \in \R^{n},
\end{equation}
with internal state $x$ (for the router: a conversation's encoded state),
control / persistent-excitation input $u$, and slowly-varying parameters
$\theta$. Linearizing along a trajectory $x^\ast$ gives the local Jacobian
\begin{equation}
\dot{\delta x} = A\,\delta x, \qquad
A = \left.\frac{\partial f}{\partial x}\right|_{x^\ast},
\end{equation}
and everything below reasons about the eigenstructure of $A$ and its symmetric
part $A_s = \tfrac12(A + A^{\top})$.

% ====================================================================
\section{The collapse-guarantee theorem}
Split the state space using $A_s$ into the \emph{near-null} subspace
$N = \operatorname{span}\{\, v_i : |\lambda_i(A_s)| < \varepsilon \,\}$ and its
orthogonal complement, the \emph{active} subspace $M$ with projector $\PM$.
Define the Lyapunov function on the active modes only,
\begin{equation}
V(x) = \tfrac12 \norm{\PM x}^2,
\end{equation}
and let the active spectral abscissa be
$\alpha = \max\{\, \lambda_i(A_s) : v_i \in M \,\}$.

\begin{theorem}[Collapse guarantee]
If $\alpha < 0$, then
\begin{equation}
\dot V \le 2\alpha V
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
\norm{\PM x(t)} \le \norm{\PM x(0)}\, e^{\alpha t}.
\end{equation}
The active modes decay exponentially at rate $|\alpha|$ and the trajectory
contracts onto the invariant null manifold $N$: collapse is guaranteed.
If $\alpha \ge 0$, some active mode is non-contracting and collapse is not
guaranteed --- the system may wander or diverge.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
On $M$, $\dot V = (\PM x)^{\top} \PM \dot x
= (\PM x)^{\top} A_s (\PM x) \le \lambda_{\max}(A_s|_M)\norm{\PM x}^2
= 2\alpha V$. Gr\"onwall gives the exponential bound; the null component is
invariant by construction of $N$.
\end{proof}

\begin{verifybox}
\textbf{Verification.} With $\alpha = -0.8$ the certificate predicts a
contraction rate of $0.8$; a real rollout shows $V$ decaying
$1.891 \to 0.004$ monotonically. The certificate is exact, not approximate.
\end{verifybox}

\section{The collapse trigger $\Sigma_0$}
\label{sec:sigma0}
$\Szero$ fires only in the genuinely underdetermined regime --- all four
conditions together (a control singularity over a structureless field):
\begin{center}
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.3}
\begin{tabular}{@{}>{$}l<{$}l@{}}
\toprule
\textrm{Condition} & \textrm{Default threshold} \\
\midrule
\norm{\nabla_x L} < \varepsilon_g & \varepsilon_g = 0.01 \\
\rank(J_f) < \rho\, n & \rho = 0.5 \\
\Sigma \textrm{ anisotropy} < \varepsilon_{\sigma} & \varepsilon_{\sigma} = 0.05 \\
\norm{\partial H/\partial u} < \varepsilon_c & \varepsilon_c = 0.01 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
where anisotropy is the normalized eigenvalue spread of $\Sigma$'s symmetric part:
\begin{equation}
\text{aniso}(\Sigma) = \frac{\sigma_{\max} - \sigma_{\min}}{\sigma_{\text{mean}}}.
\end{equation}
When \emph{all four} conditions trigger, $\Szero$ projects the state onto the
null eigenmodes of $A_s$ --- the ``42-state,'' a stable, structureless summary
the system freezes onto:
\begin{equation}
x^\ast = P x, \qquad P = V_{\mathrm{null}} V_{\mathrm{null}}^{\top},
\end{equation}
or returns the semantic null $\perp_s$ if no invariant directions exist. The
operator is implemented in \texttt{SemanticCollapseOperator.evaluate()}
(line~120, \texttt{src/cio\_sde/collapse.py}).

\section{The anti-collapse operator $\Sigma_0^{-1}$}
Where $\Szero$ projects \emph{onto} the null subspace, $\Sigma_0^{-1}$ injects
energy \emph{along} it --- the persistent-excitation principle from adaptive
control:
\begin{equation}
dx = f\,dt + dW + \Sigma_0^{-1}, \qquad
\Sigma_0^{-1} = s\cdot p \cdot (V_{\mathrm{null}}\,\xi),
\end{equation}
with $\xi$ standard normal noise, $s$ the excitation strength (default $s=0.5$),
and $p \in [0,1]$ the collapse proximity:
\begin{equation}
p = \min\bigl( p_\nabla, p_{\text{rank}}, p_{\text{aniso}}, p_{\text{ctrl}} \bigr),
\end{equation}
where each term is a soft indicator (zero when safe, one when at the threshold).
The operator is a no-op when $p=0$ (costs nothing in healthy regimes), and
gates full-strength excitation when $p \to 1$. It also re-anisotropizes the
uncertainty covariance $\Sigma$ along the recovered null directions.

\begin{verifybox}
\textbf{Verification (tests/test\_cio\_sde.py, line 221).} \texttt{test\_anti\_collapse\_suppresses\_collapse()}: on a forced collapse (zero drift, isotropic noise, near-origin state), $\Szero$ fires $40/40$ steps and state freezes; with $\Sigma_0^{-1}$ active ($s=0.5$), $\Szero$ never fires and state norm grows $\times 5$. With Σ₀⁻¹ dormant (line 235): in a structured regime, proximity $p=0$ and no excitation is added.
\end{verifybox}

\section{The early-warning scalar (the ``canary'')}
Near a bifurcation the dominant eigenvalue flattens (critical slowing down;
\cite{wissel1984, scheffer2009}). Two readouts:
\begin{align}
p_{\mathrm{unbounded}}(x) &= \frac{1}{|\Real\,\lambda_{\max}(A_s)|}
\;\xrightarrow[\text{boundary}]{}\; \infty, \\
p_{\mathrm{gate}}(x) &= \operatorname{clip}\!\Big(1 -
\tfrac{|\Real\,\lambda_{\max}|}{\varepsilon},\, 0,\, 1\Big) \in [0,1].
\end{align}
The unbounded form is the leading indicator (it diverges \emph{before}
collapse); the gated form is the bounded control signal driving
$\Sigma_0^{-1}$.

\section{Global structure: the attractor graph $G$}
The system is multistable. Collect its attractors $\{A_1,\dots,A_k\}$ (fixed
points, limit cycles, strange attractors), each with a basin
$B_i = \{\, x_0 : \lim_{t\to\infty}\phi_t(x_0) \in A_i \,\}$. Coarse-graining to
a graph $G=(V,E)$ --- nodes are attractors, edges noise/drift transitions ---
gives an induced Markov process over basins,
\begin{equation}
P_{ij}(u) = \Pr\!\big(\pi(x_{t+1}) = A_j \mid \pi(x_t) = A_i,\, u_t\big),
\end{equation}
where the partition map $\pi:\R^n \to V$ sends a state to its basin. A boundary
studded with \emph{saddles} (mixed-sign $\Real\lambda$) has stable manifolds:
safe passages one can traverse without being captured by a deep attractor.

\section{Demonstration on real router data}
\label{sec:demo}
Two experiments run the construction on the actual Lantern OS conversation log,
encoding each turn as a bounded vector
\begin{equation}
x = [\,\text{novelty},\ \text{self\_repeat},\ \text{echo},\ \text{length}\,]
\in [0,1]^4 .
\end{equation}
\texttt{router\_sigma0\_encoder.py} fits a local Jacobian per session and emits
the spiral/canary/wall readouts, $\pi$, and $P_{ij}$;
\texttt{router\_reservoir\_G.py} trains an echo-state network on one global flow
and runs it autonomously --- the reservoir \emph{becomes} $G$, its fixed points
the reconstructed attractors, fed back to the same $\Szero$ certificate.

\paragraph{Result (cross-confirmed).} This log's dynamics collapse onto a
\emph{parrot attractor}, $\text{novelty}\approx 1,\ \text{echo}\approx 0.72$ ---
a flow whose only fixed point is ``quote the prompt back.'' That is model
collapse appearing directly in the data, not inserted by hand; two unrelated
methods agreeing is the signal that it is real.

\begin{caveatbox}
\textbf{Honest caveats.} (1) The log is mostly synthetic test traffic, so the
\emph{numbers} are illustrative --- the deliverable is the pipeline. (2) The
reservoir's autonomous rollout diverges unless projected back onto the valid
$[0,1]^4$ domain; that projection \emph{is} $\pi$, not a fudge. (3) The
certificate is unreliable at boundary fixed points where the hard clamp is
non-smooth --- a log-barrier is the proper fix and is not yet implemented.
\end{caveatbox}

\section{Why this is a warning against ASI}
A system that ``comes out of its own eyes'' --- that optimizes against its own
representations with no external anchor --- is the flow $\dot x = f(x)$ where
$f$ only ever sees $x$. The certificate says such a system has two fates and no
third without outside contact:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\arabic*)]
\item \textbf{Collapse ($\Szero$):} it falls onto a degenerate,
self-consistent, dead fixed point --- the 42-state. Mirrors agreeing with
mirrors.
\item \textbf{Divergence:} with no contraction it runs to infinity (the
un-projected reservoir).
\end{enumerate}
The only stable middle --- the safe passages --- required an \emph{external
bound} (the projection back onto the real domain). \textbf{Grounding is the
safety mechanism.} Remove contact with something outside the mirrors and no
certificate keeps the system off the wall. The reservoir's collapse onto the
parrot attractor is a small, literal demonstration: a model trained only on its
own reflections converges to reflecting.
