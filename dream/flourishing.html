How Different Groups Are Doing

👥

Humans

Health Score: 53.6% · Mixed Progress

Life expectancy is rising in most countries. But mental health challenges are increasing in wealthy nations, and economic stress is widespread. Extreme poverty is declining globally—that's progress. The gap between rich and poor is growing, limiting real freedom even in free countries.

📈 Global Burden of Disease 2021 shows life expectancy gains in 80% of countries
⚖️ Freedom House 2024 reports democratic freedoms declining in some regions
🌱 UN SDG Report shows uneven progress by region
🐘

Animals & Wildlife

Welfare Score: 42.7% · Urgent Challenge

Wild animal populations have declined dramatically since 1970. Habitat loss is the biggest driver. Farm animal welfare is improving in some countries due to welfare regulations. Conservation efforts are working—species populations can recover when we act. The challenge is scale and speed.

📉 Living Planet Report 2022 shows 68% decline in vertebrate populations since 1970
🆘 IUCN Red List tracks 37,000+ threatened species
✅ Welfare science shows animals thrive with natural behaviors and space
🌳

Ecosystems & Nature

Resilience Score: 52.3% · Reversible

Biodiversity is declining, but the decline is reversible. Forests are shrinking, but deforestation rates are slowing. Ocean acidification is accelerating, and some species are already adapting. The Earth's capacity to regenerate is being tested—but conservation works when implemented consistently.

🌡️ IPCC 6th Assessment 2023 confirms climate impacts accelerating but mitigation possible
🔁 Nature 2018 meta-analysis shows conservation reverses biodiversity loss
🌲 FAO data shows deforestation rate slowing in recent years
