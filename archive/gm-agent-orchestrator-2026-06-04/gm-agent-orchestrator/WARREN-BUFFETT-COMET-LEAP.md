## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: The Buffett Case

Warren Buffett invests in businesses with **sustainable competitive advantages** (moats). Lantern exhibits five hallmarks:

1. **Switching costs:** Family invests time teaching kids to use Lantern. Switching = retraining
2. **Network effects:** Early adopter families refer friends. Word-of-mouth = free marketing
3. **Brand loyalty:** Parents trust privacy-first. No cloud tracking = emotional buy-in
4. **Cost advantage:** Lantern runs locally (no servers). Offline-first = lower unit economics
5. **Recurring revenue:** $20–$30/mo × 10 families Year 1 = proof of willingness to pay

**Buffett's definition of a great business:** Requires little capital, generates excess cash, compounds year-over-year. Lantern fits.

**Lantern Chat** — Kids talk to Claude/Gemini/Ollama, ask homework questions, get real answers
