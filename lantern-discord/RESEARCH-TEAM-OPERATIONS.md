# Research Team Operations Playbook

**Framework:** Weekly rhythm for evidence-driven decision making  
**Cadence:** Mon-Fri with Friday synthesis  
**Participants:** Founder + team (if available)

---

## Weekly Research Cycle

### Monday: Assumption Audit (9am-10am)

**Purpose:** List all active assumptions, rank by business impact

**Agenda:**
1. **Brain dump (15 min):** What are we assuming about customers, market, product, revenue?
2. **Risk ranking (30 min):** Which assumptions would kill the business if wrong?
3. **Priority assignment:** Top 5 assumptions to test this week
4. **Owner assignment:** Who owns testing each assumption?

**Output:**
- List of 10+ active assumptions
- Top 5 ranked by impact
- Owner per assumption
- Success metric for each

**Template:**

| Assumption | Owner | Impact (1-5) | Probability (1-5) | Score | Test Method |
|-----------|-------|------------|-----------------|-------|------------|
| Families want weekly check-ins | Founder | 5 | 3 | 15 | Customer interviews |
| Institutional partners trust new tools | Team | 4 | 2 | 8 | Outreach conversations |
| Care coordination reduces no-shows | Founder | 4 | 2 | 8 | Pilot data tracking |
| Privacy-first messaging resonates | Team | 3 | 3 | 9 | Message A/B test |

---

### Tuesday-Wednesday: Risk Ranking & Experiment Design (Daily Standups)

**Tuesday 9am-10am: Risk Ranking**

**Purpose:** Estimate probability and impact of each top-5 assumption being wrong

**Agenda:**
1. **Impact assessment:** If this assumption is wrong, how bad is it?
   - 5 = Fatal (company dies)
   - 4 = Severe (major pivot needed)
   - 3 = Significant (product change needed)
   - 2 = Manageable (iteration needed)
   - 1 = Minor (small adjustment)

2. **Probability assessment:** How likely is this assumption to be wrong?
   - 5 = Very likely (>60% chance)
   - 4 = Likely (40-60%)
   - 3 = Possible (20-40%)
   - 2 = Unlikely (5-20%)
   - 1 = Very unlikely (<5%)

3. **Score:** Impact Ã— Probability

4. **Testability:** Can we test this cheaply?

**Output:** Risk ranking matrix (Impact Ã— Probability)

---

**Wednesday 9am-10am: Experiment Design**

**Purpose:** Design 3-5 small experiments to test top risks

**Agenda:**
1. **Select risks:** Top 3-5 from yesterday's ranking
2. **Design tests:** What's the simplest way to validate each?
3. **Budget:** Each experiment <$200
4. **Success criteria:** What result confirms/denies assumption?
5. **Owner:** Who runs this experiment?
6. **Timeline:** Mon-Fri this week

**Experiment types:**
- **Customer interview** (5 conversations): $100 incentive + founder time
- **Feature test** (1 feature deployed): $0 cost + dev time
- **Message trial** (3 variants, 100 recipients): $0 cost + analysis time
- **Partnership pilot** (1 institution trial): $0 cost + coordination
- **Operational test** (1 workflow change): $0 cost + founder time

**Template:**

| Experiment | Assumption | Type | Success Criteria | Budget | Owner | Timing |
|-----------|-----------|------|-----------------|--------|-------|--------|
| 5 family interviews | Weekly check-ins wanted | Interview | 4/5 confirm weekly preference | $100 | Founder | Mon-Tue |
| Feature test: medication alerts | Alerts increase adherence | Feature | 70%+ activation rate | $0 | Team | Wed-Thu |
| Message A/B test | Privacy resonates | Message | Variant A outperforms B | $0 | Founder | Wed-Thu |
| Institutional call (3 clinics) | Revenue model viable | Interview | 2/3 interested in pilot | $0 | Founder | Thu |

---

### Thursday: Daily Experiment Execution

**Morning standup (15 min):** Check status of experiments in progress

**Mid-day execution (1-2 hours):** Run the experiment

**Evening debrief (15 min):** Capture outcomes, note surprises

---

### Friday: Weekly Synthesis & Reallocation (3pm-4:30pm)

**Purpose:** Synthesize learnings, update confidence, reallocate capital for next week

**Agenda:**

**Part 1: Experiment Recap (30 min)**
- Go through each Mon-Thu experiment
- What did we learn?
- Did the assumption prove true/false/nuanced?
- Update confidence score

**Part 2: Confidence Analysis (20 min)**
- Which assumptions shifted significantly?
- Tier assumptions: High/Medium/Low priority
- Identify new risks surfaced by experiments

**Part 3: Capital Reallocation (20 min)**

**Proven channels (70% budget):** >80% confidence, repeatedly validated
- Example: Institutional partnership outreach (if working)
- Allocation: 70% of next week's budget

**Emerging opportunities (20% budget):** 40-80% confidence, showing promise
- Example: Direct-to-consumer messaging (if gaining traction)
- Allocation: 20% of next week's budget

**Long-shot bets (10% budget):** <40% confidence, experimental
- Example: Employer health benefit partnerships (untested)
- Allocation: 10% of next week's budget

**Part 4: Next Week Planning (20 min)**
- Based on this week's learnings, what are top 3-5 experiments for next week?
- Budget allocation per experiment
- Owner assignment
- Success criteria locked in

**Output:**
- Weekly synthesis report (1 page)
- Updated confidence ledger
- Next week experiment roadmap
- Capital reallocation directive

## Monthly Research Cycle

### First Monday of Month: Strategy Refresh (1 hour)

**Questions:**
1. How did our confidence change this month?
2. Which risks materialized? Which surprises?
3. Is our business model still valid?
4. What's the single biggest thing we learned?
5. Does next month's strategy need adjustment?

**Output:**
- Monthly confidence trajectory
- Updated assumption list (old assumptions removed [2026-05-27] if validated, new ones added)
- Risk reassessment
- Monthly strategy memo

---

### Last Friday of Month: Full Synthesis (2 hours)

**Morning (1 hour):**
- Compile all weekly learnings
- Identify patterns (what worked, what didn't)
- Analyze confidence drift
- Document key experiments

**Afternoon (1 hour):**
- Founder reflection: Energy? Confidence? Burnout risk?
- Team feedback (if applicable)
- Next month priorities
- Share learnings with advisors

**Output:**
- Monthly synthesis document
- Updated playbook (if processes need adjustment)
- Founder wellness check
- Next month roadmap

## Research Team Roles

### Founder Role
- **Owns:** Customer interviews, institutional outreach, final decision making
- **Time commitment:** 20-25 hours/week
- **Responsibilities:** Assumption identification, risk ranking, experiment ownership, confidence tracking

### Team Member Role (if available)
- **Owns:** Product testing, message testing, operational workflows
- **Time commitment:** 15-20 hours/week
- **Responsibilities:** Experiment execution, data logging, support/troubleshooting

### Advisor Role
- **Owns:** Strategic guidance, risk assessment, pattern recognition
- **Time commitment:** 2-3 hours/month
- **Responsibilities:** Weekly summary review, monthly strategy call, decision support
