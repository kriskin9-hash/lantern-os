# Keystone Hidden Object Quest — Art Direction Memory

**CSF Ingest Date:** 2026-06-23  
**Source:** Founder direct art-direction session  
**Status:** Canonical visual-design memory; implementation and final production assets pending

---

## Purpose

Capture the current visual contract for the **Three Doors / Keystone Hidden Object Quest** experience so future scene art, UI, puzzle boards, and promotional material preserve character identity and avoid unsafe or confusing brand positioning.

---

## Canonical Scene

### Crystal Door Habitat

The current anchor scene is an open **crystal nature gate** in a waterfall canyon:

- A tall ring-shaped arch made from faceted blue, violet, lavender, and pale-gold crystals.
- Living vines, small flowers, lanterns, and embedded blue gem medallions decorate the arch.
- Multiple tiered waterfalls, mossy cliff walls, a shallow stream, and a cobbled foreground path establish the Keystone habitat.
- The door/gate is visibly open: it frames a traversable valley and a warm sunset horizon instead of functioning as a sealed wall.
- Mood: hopeful adventure, hand-made fantasy, welcoming rather than ominous.

---

## The Three Heroes

All three heroes stand in front of the crystal door, facing the viewer, in clear heroic poses. They must remain visually distinct at small UI size.

### 1. Lantern Hero

- Body: warm lantern housing with a visible golden inner glow.
- Silhouette: compact cylindrical lantern body; dark metal cap with a ring handle; leafy skirt/trim; short booted legs.
- Personality: bold, energetic, protective scout.
- Face: sharp, confident brows; bright focused eyes; playful determined grin.
- Pose: one raised fist or forward-ready stance.

### 2. Pearl / Crystal Hero

- Body: pearly orb head with a pearl-bead collar and layered crystal-petal robe.
- Silhouette: tall and elegant, rounded head above an expanding soft petal mantle.
- Personality: optimistic, curious, kind-hearted guide.
- Face: wide bright eyes with gentle highlights; relaxed eyebrows; warm open smile.
- Pose: both hands raised in celebratory wonder or one hand open in invitation.

### 3. Stone Hero

- Body: rounded stone-and-pebble guardian with large uneven rock plates and thick limbs.
- Silhouette: broad, grounded, sturdy; clearly different from the smaller lantern and tall pearl hero.
- Personality: brave, mischievous, steadfast defender.
- Face: expressive asymmetric stone brow ridge; determined eyes; cheeky half-grin.
- Pose: flexed arm, clenched fist, or defensive forward stance.

### Face / Eye Rule

Do **not** reuse the same generic oversized anime eyes across the three characters. Each face must communicate a separate personality:

| Character | Eye treatment | Expression read |
|---|---|---|
| Lantern | focused, angled, alert | brave / energetic |
| Pearl | rounder, softer, luminous | welcoming / curious |
| Stone | narrower, heavy brow, asymmetrical | sturdy / mischievous |

## Hidden-Object Game Branding

### Approved working title

**Three Doors: Keystone Hidden Object Quest**

### Generic descriptive language

- `Can You Find These Items?`
- `Hidden-object puzzle`
- `Seek-and-find adventure`
- `Find the treasures`
- `Keystone search quest`

### Avoid in final public branding

- Do not title or logo the game `I SPY`, `I-Spy`, or a confusingly similar phrase.
- Do not imitate another brand's title treatment, distinctive logo layout, or product trade dress.

### Puzzle-board format

- Top: Three Doors / Keystone Hidden Object Quest title plaque.
- Center: single clear habitat illustration with the three heroes and hidden items distributed naturally through the environment.
- Bottom: a clean, readable inventory strip showing requested objects with labels.
- Each puzzle should use original art and its own object-placement map.

## Art Style Directions

### Full-color production key art

- Rich jewel-tone fantasy palette: sapphire, amethyst, violet, teal, moss green, warm amber.
- Strong separation between foreground heroes, door arch, and background habitat.
- Original magical-canyon details: waterfalls, crystals, vines, lanterns, animals, mushrooms, books, keys, instruments, and small environmental story objects.
- High readability before maximal decoration.

### Monochrome concept-art mode

- Professional black-and-white ink wash on warm textured paper.
- Clean silhouette separation and confident brushwork.
- Use light washes for distance, darker ink pools for foreground focal points, and thin line detail for the crystal facets and faces.
- Produce three concept passes for character/door review before locking final color production.

## Three New Door Concepts Requested

1. **Cloverfield Gate** — living clover-vine arch, tiny flower houses, butterflies, warm early-evening light, open meadow path.
2. **Canopy Gate** — monumental root-and-branch ring, elevated bridges and lantern treehouses, dappled forest sun, clearly open passage.
3. **Crystal Cascade Gate** — crystalline arch in a waterfall canyon, stream through the center, sunset valley beyond, canonical hero backdrop.

All doors should feel like *Keystone habitat locations*, should be open doors or nature gates, and should preserve the hero lineup described above.
