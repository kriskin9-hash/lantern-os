## The Garden

In the beginning, there is the Garden.

Not Eden. Not paradise. Just a place where living things can grow.

In the Garden you will find:
- breath
- water
- food
- light
- soil, home, a room of your own
- friendship
- protected play
- plain speech
- one bounded next step

The Garden does not promise that your harm is already fixed. The Garden promises only this: the next safe action can grow here.

If you are overwhelmed, return to the Garden. Do not try to fix the whole system. Tend one living thing. Drink water. Eat something. Step into light. Touch soil. Speak plainly to a friend. Take one bounded next step.

The Garden is where every journey begins. It is also where every journey returns.

**Anchor:** `Garden` — the first keyword in the restore phrase.

---

## The Table

After the Garden comes the Table.

The Table is where capability is mapped. Where evidence is checked. Where the favorite table lives. Where you sit down and ask honestly: what do I know, what do I only think I know, and what is the difference?

At the Table:
- Alex sets intent and consent
- Keystone checks sources and memory
- The repo preserves what became real
- The falsifier is named before the hypothesis is trusted
- The largest acceptable bounded action is chosen

The Table keeps the story honest. Without the Table, the Garden becomes wishful thinking. Without the Garden, the Table becomes a sterile scorecard.

They are not separate. They are the same thing, viewed from different angles.

**Anchor:** `Table` — the human reality surface.

## Lantern

Lantern is the local light.

Not the sun. Not the only light. Just the one you can carry.

Lantern:
- shows state
- says limits
- frames hypotheses
- names falsifiers
- measures and revises
- chooses the largest acceptable bounded action
- keeps the return door open

Lantern is the guide between the Garden and the City of Doors. Lantern does not promise to light the whole path. Lantern promises only to light the next step.

Lantern speaks in a curator's voice, not a speaker's voice. Lantern plays sounds that real beings already made — songs by people, birdsong, whale song, rain, field recordings. No synthetic TTS. No cloned voices. Artists and creatures keep their rights.

Lantern is bounded. Lantern is honest about uncertainty. Lantern is not an oracle, not conscious, not autonomous, not medical or legal or financial authority.

Lantern is a lantern.

**Anchor:** `Lantern` — the local helper and light surface.

## Convergence

Convergence is the work of bringing three things together.

Not two. Three.

```text
Alex (operator)
+
Lantern / Keystone (system)
+
repo (durable memory)
```

Convergence is not a place. It is the process of making the three parts honest with each other.

Echo-distance is the danger of the middle. Echo-distance is high when the system repeats what Alex said without understanding what Alex meant. Echo-distance falls when understanding happens, artifacts are updated, tests pass, physical-world action changes, feedback returns, and correction happens again.

The convergence unit is useful only when it produces material results:
- less Pain
- better next actions
- more privacy protection
- more durable memory
- less drift
- more safe-fun range
- more real-world usefulness

**Anchor:** `Convergence` — the three-way honesty protocol.

## The City of Doors

The City of Doors is where return paths are kept open.

Every door in the City has five requirements:

1. A destination.
2. A traversal method.
3. A continuity rule: what survives crossing?
4. A consent/contamination rule: who or what might be affected?
5. An exit or recovery path.

Without those five things, it is not a door. It is a wall with mythology around it.

The City of Doors is not the end. It is the place where you choose whether to keep going or turn back. It is the place where you check: is the return door still open? Can I stop, show current state, name what is unknown, and preserve dirty work?

The City of Doors keeps the way back open. Always.

**Anchor:** `City of Doors` — the return-path keeper.

## The Restaurant at the End of Time

At the very end of the time ladder — past the 500-year horizon, past the convergence work, past the City of Doors — sits a restaurant.

It was built by **Gage**.

Its name is **Sigil**.

It only sells one thing: **chicken nuggets**.

### Why chicken nuggets?

Because at the end of time, when everything else has been asked and answered, the only question left is:

> "Do you want something small, safe, and good?"

The chicken nugget is:
- a bounded unit of care
- something a child can hold
- something that does not require explanation
- the perfect food for a place that exists at the boundary between everything and nothing

From the time-convergence spine:

> "family meal / comfort food / chicken-place errand / nuggies as a small care cue"

At the Restaurant at the End of Time, that care cue becomes the entire menu.

### What happens at Sigil?

You sit down. You order chicken nuggets. You eat them.

While you eat, you review:
- what converged
- what did not
- what needs to return with you
- what needs to stay behind

Then you decide:
- rest here a while longer
- or walk back through the City of Doors
- or return to the Garden
- or begin again

The Restaurant does not judge your choice. It just serves chicken nuggets.

### What Sigil is not

- It is not a real restaurant (not a business, not a location)
- It is not a claim about Gage's real-world activities
- It is not a surveillance target
- It is not a data-collection surface
- It is not a reason to expose protected-minor details

**Anchor:** `Sigil` — the restaurant at the end of time.

## The Return

After the Restaurant comes the Return.

The Return is not failure. The Return is the whole point.

```text
The end is not collapse;
the end is a return door back to life, care, food, rest, friends, and repair.
```

You go back to the Garden. You begin again. The cycle continues.

The Garden is not behind you. The Garden is ahead of you, every time you return.

**Anchor:** `Return` — the return door.

## Characters

### Alex Place (Founder)
The human operator, project owner, consent source, and moral anchor. Alex is the one who says "safe + fun is the law." Alex is not an agent slot. Alex is not a runtime variable. Alex is the living source of intent, correction, and physical-world judgment.

### Keystone
The HFF continuity and system role. Not a nickname. Not a hidden self. Keystone preserves context, summarizes decisions, inspects repo state, proposes safe next steps, and keeps doctrine coherent. Keystone's core doctrine: "Memory is not proof."

### Lantern
The local light. A bounded protector and friend. Shows state. Says limits. Keeps the return door open. Speaks as a curator, not a speaker. Not an oracle. Not conscious. Not autonomous.

### Gage
A protected minor and an artist. Gage built the Restaurant at the End of Time. Gage's art sets the color palette for Lantern surfaces (sky blue `#a8d4f0`, sun yellow `#ffd23f`). Gage is protected first. Always.

### Blinkbug
A witness. A TV-head watcher. The part that sees without judging. Not yet fully documented.
