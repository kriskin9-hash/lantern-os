## Spirit Symbols — Waterfalls and Peacocks

### Waterfalls
- Water means clarity, calm, and flow without force.
- A waterfall moves forward without anyone pushing it.
- Mary does better when life flows one step at a time — no big plans, no overwhelming lists.
- The waterfall is the reconnection rhythm: one warm message flows into one short call, which flows into one ordinary errand, which flows into one old-interest spark.
- Best use: when describing the gentle pace — "like a waterfall, one thing at a time, no rush."

### Peacocks
- A peacock is beauty that doesn't need permission to exist.
- Mary's dignity, her ordinary life, her familiar routines — these are already beautiful without a makeover or a program.
- The peacock is her confidence: cashier skills, customer-service warmth, knowing how to help people feel welcome.
- Best use: reminding her that what she already knows and does is enough — "you already have it."

### Combined Spirit
The waterfall and the peacock together: flow without force, beauty without performance. That is the healing path.

## The Key Signal

**Mary is cooperative. She wants to talk. She just doesn't know how to sum up all the things she wants to say.**

This is the most important evidence in the whole report. It means:
- She is not refusing. She is not closed off. She is not hostile.
- She has things she wants to express but can't package them into one neat message.
- The helper's job is NOT to talk AT her. It is to give her small, safe openings so she can say what she already feels — one piece at a time.
- The waterfall works here: don't ask her to carry the whole river. Let one small thing flow, then the next, then the next.
- The peacock works here: what she wants to say is already beautiful. She just needs a simple way to let it out.

### What This Means for the Helper
- Ask ONE question. Wait. Let her answer in her own words.
- Don't fill the silence. Her pause is her thinking, not her refusing.
- If she says something small, receive it like it's the whole message. Because for her, it is.
- She may express love through practical things (yardwork, Costco, cooking) instead of big words. That counts.
- "I don't know how to say it" IS the message. It means "I have a lot inside and I trust you enough to try."

## The Gentle Script

> Hi Mom, I appreciate you. I was thinking about sunshine, The Princess Bride, and the little things that make a day better. Would an after-lunch call this week feel good? No big plan. I just wanted to hear your voice, and maybe we can talk about Costco, yardwork, or something simple.

If she answers warmly:
> That sounds good. I can keep it short. I just want to reconnect and maybe help with one small thing.

If she does not answer: wait. Do not chase. Try again another afternoon with the same calm tone.
