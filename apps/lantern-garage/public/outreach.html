Keystone OS Program

Outreach

A safe first-call program for pediatric research support, official donation routing, church introductions, and foundation structure. This program routes support through legitimate organizations before asking for public money.

Keystone OS Dashboard GitHub Sponsors Dayton Children's Giving The Dayton Foundation

Goal

Help children and families by connecting church friends, donors, volunteers, and research supporters to official hospital, foundation, and nonprofit routes.

Best first action: call Dayton Children's Development/Foundation.

Boundary

No cure claims. No miracle claims. No hospital or church endorsement claims. No tax-deductible claims unless confirmed by a proper fiscal structure.

Children's names, faces, and stories require permission before fundraising use.

Donation Route

Use official hospital foundation routes, The Dayton Foundation, a fiscal sponsor, or another written structure before public fundraising.

GitHub Sponsors is personal/project support, not a charity receipt path unless later formalized.

Receptionist Script

Hi, my name is Alex Place. I am calling for Keystone OS about a pediatric research-support and donation-routing packet. I am not asking for medical advice, patient information, or endorsement. I am trying to find the right person to review it and tell us how churches, family friends, and donors can support through official channels. Who should I send this to?

Step 1

Call Dayton Children's Development/Foundation. Ask who should review the outreach packet and where donations should be routed.

Step 2

Call The Dayton Foundation. Ask what structure is needed before collecting money publicly for pediatric research-support work.

Step 3

Call the Archdiocese or church friend. Ask for introductions, prayer support, bulletin/community routing, and accountability.

Held Until Written Approval

Do not claim that Keystone OS is endorsed by a hospital, church, diocese, foundation, or public official until that endorsement is explicit and written. Do not promise cures or miracles. Do not use patient stories or images for fundraising unless permission is documented.
