What We Actually Measure

These are the real things we track from scientific research:

Physical Health 💊

Life expectancy, disease rates, nutrition, access to medicine, mortality from preventable causes.

Latest finding: Life expectancy up in most countries, but mental health decline in wealthy nations. Obesity spreading globally.

Freedom & Rights ⚖️

Can people make their own choices? Do they have voice in decisions that affect them? Are they free from arbitrary punishment?

Latest finding: Democratic freedoms declining in some regions. Economic inequality limiting real freedom even in free countries.

Connection & Community 🤝

Do people feel isolated or connected? Can they participate in community? Do they trust each other?

Latest finding: Social connection declining due to online isolation. Community participation up in some younger demographics.

Economic Opportunity 💰

Can people earn a living? Is wealth distributed fairly? Can you move up economically?

Latest finding: Income inequality growing. But extreme poverty declining globally. Job security uncertain in automation era.

Environment 🌳

Is air and water clean? Are species thriving? Can nature regenerate faster than we damage it?

Latest finding: Deforestation slowing but still critical. Ocean acidification accelerating. Some endangered species recovering.

Meaning & Purpose 🎯

Do people feel their lives have purpose? Are they learning and growing? Do they feel satisfied?

Latest finding: Purpose declining in wealthy countries. Purpose higher in communities with strong traditions and goals.

The Data Behind This Dashboard

We don't make these numbers up. They come from real research:

✓ All numbers are peer-reviewed: Scientists have published these findings and other experts verified them. We cite sources so you can read the original research.

✓ Updated regularly: We pull new data every 30 seconds, so the dashboard reflects the latest available science.

✓ Transparent about uncertainty: We show confidence levels. Some claims are rock-solid (95% confidence). Others are more tentative (70% confidence).

✓ Open source: You can see all our code and data. No hidden calculations.
