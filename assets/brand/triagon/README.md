# Triagon — low-poly signal mascot

> Triagon is the little three-point observer that turns scattered signals into one clear direction.

An original mascot for the Western / geometric-facing brand: a friendly, low-poly
digital familiar that feels retro-computational and easy to draw. **Triagon is a
living three-point signal** — it gathers scattered observations, turns them toward
one another, and emits a clean answer. Designed to live near the Σ₀ / convergence
system (Observe → Remember → Reason → Act → Verify → Converge).

## Anatomy

- **Head** — broad floating triangular-prism / rounded arrowhead, ~60% of the visual mass. Top **bevel facet** + front plane + a cyan **chin emitter** (the clean emitted answer).
- **Face** — flat dark display panel inset in the front plane; two wide-set **split-diamond eyes**; no beak, snout, or mouth by default.
- **Core** — a compact hovering **tetrahedral** core beneath the head, two visible faces, on **three short glowing struts**.
- **Stabilizers** — **three** wedge fins at 120° (tripod silhouette — no arms/legs).
- **Tail** — **three** linked triangular plates that orbit behind, tracing a loose triangle. This is the signature motion mark.

## The six canonical poses

1. **Idle hover** — head forward; orbiting tail plates form a triangle.
2. **Curious tilt** — head rotated 15°; one eye brighter; tilts toward a contradiction.
3. **Scanning** — three cyan rays project from the lower stabilizers.
4. **Converging** — orbiting plates pull inward into one luminous triangle behind it.
5. **Error / contradiction** — tail plates scatter; one amber eye, the other blinks.
6. **Happy answer** — display becomes an upward chevron; stabilizers spark outward.

## Drawing rules

1. **No bird silhouette** — no beak, wings, long neck, or duck posture.
2. **No humanoid body** — it hovers; the three stabilizers replace limbs.
3. **Always read as three** — three struts, three stabilizers, three tail plates.
4. **Keep it drawable in MS Paint** — thick single-/two-pixel outline, flat fills, obvious planes.
5. **Distinct from Porygon** — screen-face, floating tetra-core, radial tripod geometry, and orbiting plates; not a polygonal animal body.
